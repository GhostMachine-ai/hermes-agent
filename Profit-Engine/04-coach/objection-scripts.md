# Objection Scripts

## "Your price is too high."

**Response:**
> "Compared to what? If it's a competitor, let's look at scope side-by-side — we may be quoting different things. If it's your budget ceiling, let's figure out a phased approach that fits now and expands later."

**Never say:** "We're the best so we cost more."

---

## "We're not ready yet / bad timing."

**Response:**
> "Totally fair. What would need to change for it to be the right time? I'd rather plan around your reality than push something that doesn't land."

**Follow-up trigger:** In 30 days — "Checking in on the [blocker] you mentioned."

---

## "We're already working with [competitor]."

**Response:**
> "Good to know. Are they solving [specific problem] for you? That's usually where we get brought in alongside or after — not to replace, but to fill the gaps they don't cover."

---

## "I need to think about it / talk to my team."

**Response:**
> "Makes sense. What's the biggest open question for you or your team? I'd rather answer it now than have you sit on uncertainty."

**If they still stall:**
> "What decision do you need to make by [date] that this would affect? Happy to work backward from that."

---

## "We can do this in-house."

**Response:**
> "You probably can. The question is whether it's the best use of your team's time. What's the opportunity cost if [project] takes 3x as long internally?"

---

## "We don't have budget right now."

**Response:**
> "When does your budget cycle reset? And is there a discretionary spend path we could use for a smaller start? Sometimes a $X pilot avoids the full approval process."
